What The Various Colors And Letters Mean:

Means there is a conflict like an employee being on vacation, is absent, an overlapping schedule the employee is already assigned to, or has a repeating unavailability.

Means if the employee is assigned to the selected schedule, then the employee will either be in overtime, or be over the amount of desired hours they want to work a week plus a margin you specify (The default is 5 hours, go to Business Settings to change this.)

Means there are no conflicts or warnings and there is some overlap between a weekly repeating time the employee wants to work and the selected schedule.

Means the employee is currently assigned to the schedule. This overrides all other color flags.

(S) Means the employee is currently assigned to another schedule that has any amount of overlap with the schedule currently being considered.

(V) Means the employee is on a vacation that has any overlap with the schedule currently being considered.

(A) Means the employee is absent for a time that has any overlap with the schedule currently being considered.

(U) Means the employee has a repeating unavailability that has any overlap with the schedule being considered.

(O) Means the employee will be in overtime if assigned to this schedule.

Note: The number to the right of an employee's name in the eligible list for a given schedule is how many hours that employee is currently assigned to for that workweek.

Also! If you hover your mouse over the number of hours an employee is working, a small pop-up will tell you how many hours an employee would like to work each week.
